# Accommodation and Housing

## Overview
Finding temporary and permanent rental accommodation in London. Targeting zones 2-3 for a balance of commute time, green space, and pet-friendliness.

## Key Questions
- How long should we book temporary accommodation for? (2-4 weeks)
- Which neighbourhoods are best for pet-friendly rentals with good transport?
- What references/guarantor requirements do London landlords expect for expats?
- Furnished vs unfurnished — is it worth shipping our furniture?

## Temporary Accommodation
- **Plan**: Book a serviced apartment or Airbnb for the first 2-4 weeks
- **Budget**: £2,000-3,000 for the temporary period
- **Options researched**:
  - Sonder (serviced apartments in South Kensington, good but expensive ~£180/night)
  - The Residence, Hammersmith — pet-friendly, ~£140/night
  - Airbnb in Putney/Richmond — more space, ~£100-120/night
- **Key requirement**: Must be pet-friendly for Snoopy

## Permanent Rental — Target Areas
| Area | Zone | Avg 2-bed Rent (pcm) | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|----------------------|------|------|
| Putney | 2 | £1,800-2,200 | River walks, dog-friendly, good schools | Busy high street |
| Richmond | 3-4 | £2,000-2,500 | Richmond Park (perfect for Snoopy), leafy | Further from central |
| Wimbledon | 3 | £1,700-2,100 | Common for dog walking, District line | Can be slow commute |
| Clapham | 2 | £1,900-2,300 | Clapham Common, lively area | Noisy, less family feel |

## Rental Process for Expats
- Most landlords require 6-month rent upfront if no UK credit history
- Alternatively, a UK-based guarantor or employer reference letter
- OpenRent and Rightmove are the main platforms — set up alerts early
- **Important**: Many London rentals say "no pets" — need to specifically filter and negotiate

## Notes
- Mark's office is in the City — 30-40 min commute from zones 2-3 is acceptable
- Richmond is top choice due to Richmond Park for Snoopy, but slightly higher rents
- Check if employer will provide a relocation allowance or rental deposit support
